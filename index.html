On top of this, we held weekly technical coordination meetings with real users of our software to ensure we were consistently meeting their requirements.

Bespoke Tech Labs

Software Engineer

Mobile Applications

Whilst working at Bespoke Technology Labs, I had the chance to work on several different projects and use a wide array of modern technologies. My work was mainly used for one of our larger clients, Triangle Networks, who relied on us for a lot of their core business software.

A highlight from my software engineering work at Bespoke Technology Labs was the Android client for InDemand, a tailor-made surveying application used to collate data regarding the interest in full-fibre internet circuits all over the UK. Door-to-door canvassers were employed to use the InDemand application and bespoke web-based dashboards were generated for the companies relying on our data, namely, Vodafone and CityFibre. At the time of writing this, InDemand has recorded over 350,000 surveys, has more than 1,000,000 database entries and has revolutionised how ISP's determine where to provide full-fibre internet.

University of Plymouth

Software Engineer

Full Stack Developer

I was hand-picked by the Computer Science Faculty at my university (in conjunction with the University of Plymouth's Medical School) to engineer a diary replacement for patients of multiple sclerosis (in association with BRiMS). Development spanned several months and started with meeting MS patients regularly to truly understand the problem space and plan for any obstacles that may appear.

This software solution's main purpose was to track how many times patients were falling per day and the amount of injuries occurring as a result. The outcome was a robust relational database (SQL Server) which was connected to a cross-platform mobile application (C# Xamarin) with the potential of reaching a test group of up to 900 MS patients. This allowed medical professionals easy access to valuable data, meaning an illness with little known about it could be explored in more depth.
